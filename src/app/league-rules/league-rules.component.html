League Rules

General Information

  • This is a 10 week, 4 Point Pitch League
  • There are 9 weeks of regular season play
  • The league consists of 2 man teams, playing a total of 5 games head-to-head
  • Each game starts at 11 points, the first team to reach 0 wins the match.
  • We play for high, low, jack and game. No tens are in the deck.
  • You must capture the jack to receive the point, but do not have to capture low.

Where\When do we play?

  • We play at the Duryea VFW every Tuesday at 6:30 pm. - if that is too early for a team to play, please consult the league roster, contact the team and coordinate a better time to play.
  • The VFW is located at 492 Stephenson St, Duryea, PA 18642.
  • The VFW kitchen is open on Tuesday's, food is available for purchase if needed
  • Have nowhere else to go? After your scheduled match, hang out and play a few pick-up games!

What's the Cost?

  • There is a weekly costs of 10 GIL per man (20 GIL per team). Please pay each week on time. You are also responsible for the 10 GIL weekly fee, even if you are not present
  • Teams are also charged 1 GIL per total "ups" at the end of all 3 games that must be payed the evening of the match.
  • Ups are owed only for the losing team of that game. For example, if team 1 is playing team 10, team 1 has 4 ups for a game, and team 10 has 8 ups. If team 10 wins, team 1 owes 4 GIL, team 10 does not owe for that game.
  • To purchase beer\drinks, you may be required to purchase a social membership to the VFW. It is an annual cost of $15, but a great way to support the local chapter. You do not have to be a military veteran to acquire a membership.

What's the "money" for?

  • 100% of all "up's" go towards an end of season party
  • An additional 400 GIL from weekly dues are kept in reserve towards the end of season party
  • A small amount of funds go towards decks of cards, scoring sheets and other items required to run the league
  • The top 4 teams play in an end of season tournament (see playoff's section for more details). Payout's are ESTIMATED as follows:
  • 1st place - 800 GIL
  • 2nd place - 400 GIL
  • 3rd place - 250 GIL
  • 4th place - 125 GIL
  • We will also keep aside at least 225 GIL for a 6 team losers bracket, also to be played out the final week of the season.

What if I can't make it?

  • If you cannot attend, attempt to find an alternate player to fill in for you, or contact the other team - NOT THE COMMISH to reschedule.
  • Please be mindful of other peoples time, if you cannot make a match, or will be late, please contact the other team and let them know.
  • You are also responsible for the 10 GIL weekly fee, even if you are not present
  • A full time player on another team cannot be a substitute, but check the league contact list for specified league alternate's, or contact the commissioner if you have another partner you'd like to have sit in.
  • All missed games must be made up by the final week of the season. It's always possible to make games up after your weekly scheduled match, but if necessary meet another night at a time that is convenient for both teams. It's always an option to perform make-up games after a regularly scheduled match, but remember that other teams may be attempting to perform make-up games as well, so complete your matches as soon as possible! Remember, the league depends on all scheduled matched being completed on time.
  • You can download a printable scoring sheet in the downloads section of the website if needed. If not available, please track the date, scorekeeper, total "ups" for each team, scores for each match, and a final score. Present to the commissioner(s) once complete.

Cheating

Don't Do It

Cheating is a serious offense.. and seriously uncool. Don't signal, don't tip cards, don't throw games. If a team is suspected of cheating, please contact a commissioner directly stating the infraction(s). If a team is cheating, the level of offense and penalty will be decided by the commissioner(s), which can vary from forfeiture of single games, to matches, to an entire season.

In Game Rules

  • Choosing the Dealer - Cards are initially shuffled, one card is flipped over for each player. The highest card (or first Ace) will be the initial dealer for the first game. Teams can also choose to make the "lowest" card the scorekeeper. After a game is won, the next player who would have received deal on the winning team will become dealer for the start of the new game.
  • Misdeals – A misdeal shall be defined as when the dealer either deals too many or too few cards or when a card is turned or flipped over by dealer before first card is played. The deal must be passed to the player to the left. If card is flipped over by a player other than dealer, the cards shall be dealt over and shall not be passed. A dealer misdeal will lead to the dealing team going up for the points that have been bid, or if the bidding has not begun yet, the dealing team will go up 2 points.
  • No Forced Bids – When no bid is tendered, the dealer may choose to not play out the hand, but the team will go up 2 points.
  • Talking across the table – After the cards are dealt and the bidding is in process, players should not talk across the table. For example, a player should not express dismay at their hand to encourage their partner to bid. Players should not coach their partners as to which cards to throw. It is up to each player to keep track of what point cards are played or as to what suit trump is - it is never appropriate to ask. Any player may ask the score, however it is inappropriate to announce the score across table to your partner as to be construed as encouragement to bid.
  • Renegs – A renege is defined as when a player does not follow suit when they have that suit in their hand. The team that has reneged will go up for the points that have been bid and the opposing team receives the points bid.
  • Playing out of turn – This is defined as when a player pitches out of turn. Opposing teams receives points bid and deal is passed (this does not count for very last card as it does not affect other cards pitched).
  • Bidding to go out – It is not necessary to bid a team to win. The first team to 0 shall receive the win. If both teams reach 0 in the final hand, the team that bid receives the wins.
  • Counting for game - Cards shall be counted face up on the table. Opposing teams can request a re-count if they feel necessary.
  • Finished Players – Enjoy the games and have fun! However, it is not appropriate to give advice to individuals who are still playing or point out mistakes that have been made. Please avoid any commentary until all matches are complete.
  • At no time can a player throw in his cards during a game. Example – a player has no trump and “phone numbers” for cards, so he throws in all remaining cards in his had early in the game. It’s bad etiquette and forbidden due to good sportsmanship rules.
  • If you do not agree or understand any of the rules, please clarify with the co-commissioners prior to starting any games.
  • For any rules not defined explicitly herein, consult the league Co-Commissioner's, they have final say

Playoffs

  • After all regular season weeks have concluded, top 4 teams based on winning % advance to the playoffs.
  • Tie-Breaker #1 - If 2 teams are tied via winning percentage for 1 playoff position (example: 4th seed), their regular season head-to-head score determine's who advances.
  • Tie-Breaker #2 - If 3 or more teams are tied via winning percentage for playoff position(s), head-to-head record is ignored, the teams will face off in a 3 game playoff (example: if 3 teams are tied, the 3 teams will face each other in a 2 vs. 2 vs. 2 matchup). The first team to 3 wins advances. If multiple playoff seeds are being decided, the first winning team moves on, the other teams remain playing until the next team has reached 3 wins. The head-to-head-to-head matchup will be scheduled as needed,but must be completed by the start of Week 10 (playoff week). Teams should coordinate and find a time to play amongst themselves, but contact the commissioner(s) should scheduling become a problem. Failure to complete the playoff matchup in a timely fashion will result in forfeiture.
  • Seeding - Teams are seeded based on winning % (followed by when they got into the playoffs, see Tie-Breaker #2). There are 2 rounds of playoffs, Round 1: Seed #1 vs. Seed #4 and Seed #2 vs Seed #3. Round 2: Winner's advance to vie for 1st and 2nd place, Loser's advance to vie for 3rd and 4th place. All matches are a best-of-3 head-to-head matchup.
  • We will have a "loser's tournament" at the end of year party for all teams not ranked in the top 4 at the end of the season.
